About

For this campaign, I wanted to catch people's attention while they walk by and cause them to stop and take a closer look. Alzheimer's Disease is a confusing world both for those who have it and those who care for someone who does. The posters reflect an abstract view on what it is like to be affected by Alzheimer's.

My Favorite Part:

I really enjoyed "getting weird" with the typography. It was a unique chance to play with the type in a more compositional, abstract way rather than direct communication. The top portion of each poster communicates a fact about the disease, but from my perspective I think it is powerful regardless of whether or not the user can decipher it.

What Challenged Me:

The hardest part was setting up the photography. It was a challenge finding older adults who both fit the profile and were willing to have their picture taken. I tried taking some photos of the street, but ended up setting up photoshoots with each of the actors who graciously obliged.
