Book Design

Face To Face With The Gorilla King

Skills Used

  • Hand Lettering
  • Ideation
  • Layout

About

This is a children’s book targeted at boys in grades 4–6. Kids at that age generally don't want to feel like they are reading a “kids book”, so I created something that felt a little more mature and contemporary. I handlettered the front cover which is then diecut to reveal the gorilla's face staring back at you. There is a magical piece of wood in the story, so that has been incorporated throughout the design.

My Favorite Part:

I liked doing the handlettering for the cover. It was a different style than I had ever done before and was a fun change. I started on paper by sketching out the words within the gorilla's face shape and then transferred it into digital.

What Challenged Me:

Because of the complexity in the lettering and diecut concept, I wasn't able to make a real life version of this book. Therefore, I had to find a way to mock it up digitally, but still show the depth in the cover. It took a lot of trial and error to come up with a way to present it that looked realistic.
